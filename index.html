Notes · how it works
What this system does

Once a day after the close it scores BTC into one of three market states, runs the matching engine, and outputs a trade. It uses four classic indicators: a custom RSI(14), SMA20 / SMA50 (20- and 50-day averages), MACD (12/26 EMAs and its 9-day signal line), and Bollinger Bands (20-day, ±2 standard deviations).

Step 1 — which market state?

TREND: Bollinger bands are wide and price is making steady progress -> Engine X.

RANGING (Chop): RSI sits near the middle (40-60), barely moving, and the bands are narrow -> Engine Y.

TOXIC: the 14-day range is bigger than 15% of price but price ends up back where it started (no follow-through) -> stand aside, hold nothing.

Engine X — trend rules (exact)

Goes LONG only when ALL of these line up: price closes above its 20-day average; the MACD line is above its signal line; the RSI is above its own 20-day average; the fast EMA is above the slow EMA with MACD positive; and price is above the 50-day average.

Goes SHORT in the mirror: price below the 20-day average, MACD below signal, RSI below its average (but still >35 to avoid panic bottoms), price below the 50-day average.

Exit: a trailing stop that ratchets behind the trade's best price — set at 95% of the high once the trade is up 20%+, 93% if up 10-20%, otherwise 91.5%. It closes when price falls back through that stop or through the middle of its recent channel.

Engine Y — range rules (exact)

Active only in a ranging market, and it does the opposite of X — it fades extremes back to the middle:

BUY when the RSI was at or below 45 and is now turning back up, AND price is touching the lower Bollinger band (within ~2% of it).

SELL / short when the RSI was at or above 55 and is now turning back down, AND price is touching the upper Bollinger band (within ~2%).

Exit: when price snaps back to the middle of the channel.

The cut-loss and the bear tight-stop

On top of the engine exit, every trade carries a hard stop: 6% from entry, tightened to 3% on counter-trend longs in a confirmed bear (200-day average falling AND price below it) — fighting a downtrend is cut fast; with-trend shorts keep room.

Live, this stop is a resting order on the exchange, so it fires intraday the instant price hits it — you are protected 24/7, not only at the daily close.

Sizing, leverage & the confidence score

Confidence (0-100) rates how strong a setup is versus the engine's own history (trend strength, how far price has stretched, channel position, volatility). It sets the bet size: High -> 60%, Med -> 50%, Low -> 45% of equity as margin. Leverage is 5x on longs, 2x on shorts.

The ROI curve reproduces the full backtest (this exact logic) 2014-today — Binance from Aug-2017, CoinGecko daily before. Most of the gains came in 2015-2021; the last ~2 years are weak, and a real ~-50% drawdown is part of the ride. Simulated — a guide, not a promise.
