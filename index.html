Notes · how it works
What the three market states mean

Every day the model sorts the market into one of three moods:

TREND — price is moving with conviction in one direction, measured by the Bollinger Band width relative to price. Wide bands plus steady progress means a trend. Engine X takes over.

RANGING — price drifts sideways in a band, no clear direction (RSI stuck near the middle, bands narrow). Engine Y takes over.

TOXIC / OFF — a big range but no follow-through: violent chop that whipsaws both ways. The model stands aside and holds nothing rather than get chopped up.

Engine X — riding trends

Engine X only works when the market is trending. The idea: join a strong move and stay in until it clearly turns.

It goes long when price is above its 20-day average and momentum (MACD) is positive; short in the mirror image. Once in, a trailing stop ratchets behind price — the more profit a trade builds, the tighter the stop gets, locking gains in. It exits when price falls back through that stop or the middle of its recent channel.

Engine Y — fading the range

Engine Y only works when the market is ranging, and does the opposite of X: instead of chasing, it fades extremes.

When price stretches down to the lower Bollinger Band and turns back up, it buys; when price pushes up to the upper band and rolls over, it sells. It bets price snaps back toward the middle of the range — what usually happens while no trend is in control.

Target and cut-loss

Target is the profit objective; cut-loss is the line where the trade is abandoned to limit damage.

For trend trades (Engine X) the real protection is the trailing stop above — it moves with price. For range trades (Engine Y) the target is the middle of the range and the cut-loss sits just beyond the band, because if price runs past the band the "range" assumption was wrong.

Reality check: these levels are checked once a day at the close. A real leveraged position can be stopped out intraday before the close — so treat simulated results as a best case, not a promise.

What "confidence" means here

Confidence is a 0–100 score rating how strong this setup is versus all past setups of the same engine. It blends trend strength, how far price has extended, where price sits in its channel, and how calm volatility is.

In testing, high-confidence entries earned more on average than low-confidence ones, so it's a useful filter — but an indicator only. The buy/sell decision does not depend on it.
